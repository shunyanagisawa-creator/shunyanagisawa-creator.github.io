Background & Expertise

Shun Yanagisawa is Director and Head of Futures, OTC Clearing & FX Prime Brokerage at Citi Japan, with over twenty years at the frontier of global financial market infrastructure. His career spans J.P. Morgan (eleven years), Citi Japan, and early ventures in Japan's dot-com era — establishing a distinctive vantage point at the convergence of market structure, regulatory policy, and systems design.

He is a central figure in Japan's clearing and rates ecosystem, serving on the boards and working groups of FIA Japan, JSCC, ISDA Japan, and TFX. His work has directly shaped landmark developments including JSCC Agency Repo Clearing, cross-product margining, TONA futures, and the CFTC No-Action Letter enabling US hedge funds to access JPY IRS clearing via JSCC.

Beyond technical expertise, Yanagisawa approaches market infrastructure as a profound human question: how trust is engineered at scale, and what clearing architecture reveals about civilization's relationship with risk and uncertainty. This perspective informs both his professional work and his writing — from specialist reference works to speculative fiction.

柳沢 俊は、シティグループ証券株式会社にてフューチャーズ・OTCクリアリング・FXプライムブローカレッジ部門のDirectorを務める。20年超にわたり国際金融市場インフラの最前線に立ち、J.P. Morgan（11年）、Citi、そして日本のドットコム時代の起業経験を経て、市場構造・規制政策・システム設計の交差点に独自の視座を確立してきた。

Japan's Rates Market Sees Liquidity Boost on Policy Shift

Futures & Options World · 9 April 2026 · Karry Lai · Sole Interview Subject

In-depth analysis on the structural transformation of Japan's yen rates market, including:

  • BOJ policy normalisation driving yen swap clearing volumes up 8× since 2021 — JPY 229 trillion daily average in 2025, with a record single-day volume of JPY 146 trillion (Dec 4, 2025)
  • CFTC No-Action Letter (September 2025) enabling US hedge funds — Citadel, Millennium, ExodusPoint et al. — to clear JPY IRS via JSCC; non-Japanese client base expanded to 65 firms
  • Non-resident participants growing from 44% to 65% of cleared notional (Q4 2025)
  • US SEC mandatory Treasury Repo clearing rules and the structural challenge for Japanese megabanks with US branches
  • JGB 10-year futures open interest exceeding JPY 170 trillion by end-2025; 20-year JGB futures open interest surpassing 40,000 lots (Jan 2026)

Wealth Stewardship 資産運営の思想

Designing Order in Family Wealth Systems 家族資産における秩序の設計

The same question that drives my work in clearing infrastructure — how humans design trust and order within complex, uncertain systems — applies with equal force to family wealth stewardship.

クリアリングインフラで問い続けてきた問い—— 「複雑で不確実な世界に、人間はいかにして秩序と信頼を設計するか」——は、 家族資産の運営においても同じ力で働く。

A family office is not primarily an investment vehicle. It is a governance architecture: who decides what, who holds which information, and how that knowledge survives the people who created it.

ファミリーオフィスは本質的に、投資の器ではない。 誰が何を決め、誰がどの情報を持ち、 その知識がどう次の世代へ渡るかを設計する、 ガバナンスの構造体である。

Japan's private wealth ecosystem lacks this operational layer. The gap is not in investment products. It is in structured operations, governance design, and succession readiness — the unglamorous infrastructure that determines whether a family's wealth outlasts the generation that built it.

日本のプライベートウェルスにはこの運営レイヤーが薄い。 欠けているのは投資商品ではなく、構造化された運営・ ガバナンス設計・承継への準備—— 家族の富が、それを築いた世代を超えて生き残れるかどうかを 決める、地味だが本質的なインフラである。

This is the same design problem I encounter in clearing: building systems that hold when the humans who built them are no longer in the room.

これは、クリアリングで向き合ってきた設計問題と同じ構造を持つ。 それを作った人間がもはやその場にいないときでも、 機能し続けるシステムをどう設計するか。
